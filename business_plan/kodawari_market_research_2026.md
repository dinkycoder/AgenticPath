# KodaWari Market Research Report
## Product-Market Fit Analysis & Strategic Refactoring Recommendations
**Date:** May 2026
**Classification:** Strategic — internal use only

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The market for agentic AI education is real, large, and structurally underserved at the depth KodaWari is designed to deliver. The opportunity is not in competing with DeepLearning.AI or Coursera on breadth — it is in owning the vertical that they structurally cannot: **production-grade, character-driven, deeply opinionated multi-agent system design for engineers who already know the basics and need to go further.**

The single most important market signal: **hiring for AI agents is revealing a catastrophic lack of foundational engineering depth.** Candidates list "AI Expert" and "Agent Architect" on resumes, but most lack the systems thinking required for production. That gap is KodaWari's entire reason to exist.

## SECTION 1: MARKET SIZE & GROWTH

### The Agentic AI Market
- Global agentic AI market: **$7.6B in 2025 → projected $236B by 2034** (CAGR ~44%)
- AI Agents market specifically: **$7.63B in 2025 → $182.97B by 2033** (CAGR 49.6% — Grand View Research)
- Multi-Agent System market: **$6.3B in 2025 → $184.8B by 2034** (Dimension Market Research)
- AI in Education market: **$7.05B in 2025 → $32.27B by 2030** (Grand View Research)

**What this means for KodaWari:** The underlying technology market is growing at nearly 50% CAGR. The education market for this technology is growing at ~35% CAGR. Both curves are steep. The window to establish a category-defining brand is 12–18 months before the space becomes commoditized.

### The Education Supply Gap
- DeepLearning.AI's Agentic AI course: **$25-30/month** (membership model). Revenue: $52.8M in 2025.
- Maven cohort courses on agents/MCP/evals: **$2,250 for 6 weeks** — with documented 15,000+ paid enrollments at that price point before going free.
- MIT/Oxford $2,500 agentic AI curriculum released free on GitHub — confirming demand at premium price points but also commoditization pressure on generic content.
- Top 5 Agentic AI course list (YouTube, 2026): dominated by Coursera, IBM, DeepLearning.AI, LangChain Academy — **none with narrative, character, or visual design differentiation.**

**The gap:** Every competitor teaches *what* to build and *how*. None teach *why it matters*, *what mastery feels like*, or *how an engineer internalizes judgment* — which is precisely what the market is failing to produce.

## SECTION 2: BUYER DEMOGRAPHICS & PSYCHOLOGY

### Who Is Actually Buying AI Agent Education

From Stack Overflow Developer Survey 2025 (33,000+ respondents):

- **84%** of developers use or plan to use AI tools — up from 76% in 2024
- **51%** of professional developers use AI tools *daily*
- **Early career devs (1-5 yrs) use AI daily at the highest rate: 55.5%** — they are the most tool-dependent
- **Experienced devs (10+ yrs) show the most caution:** highest distrust rate (20% "highly distrust"), lowest trust in complex task handling
- **52% of developers don't use AI agents or stick to simpler tools** — the "not yet" market is massive
- **Only 3% "highly trust" AI tool output** — developers know they need to understand the system, not just use it

From LangChain State of Agent Engineering Survey (1,300+ professionals):
- Primary audience: engineers, product managers, business leaders
- Key pain: moving from "demo works" to "production works" — **the gap between prototype and deployed system**

From Reddit r/AI_Agents (hiring signal):
> *"Almost every candidate now lists 'AI Expert' or 'Agent Architect' on their resume, but many lack the engineering depth required for production."*

**Buyer psychology breakdown:**

| Segment | What They Want | What They Fear | Price Tolerance |
|---|---|---|---|
| Early career (1-5 yrs) | Credentials, speed, job relevance | Being left behind | $200-800 |
| Mid career (5-10 yrs) | Depth, production patterns, judgment | Looking incompetent | $500-2,500 |
| Senior/architect (10+ yrs) | Peer-level discourse, nuance, systems thinking | Wasting time on basics | $1,000-5,000+ |
| Non-technical leaders | Vocabulary, frameworks, strategic understanding | Being out-of-depth with their own teams | $500-2,000 |

**KodaWari's primary sweet spot: mid-career developers (5-10 years) who use AI tools but cannot yet architect production multi-agent systems with confidence.** This is the segment with the highest unmet need, highest willingness to pay for depth, and highest professional anxiety about the gap between their current capability and what hiring is now demanding.

## SECTION 4: WHAT THE MARKET IS ACTUALLY DEMANDING

### Signal 1: Depth over breadth
The market is drowning in introductory content. The complaint is universal — "I've done 10 courses and still can't ship a production agent." KodaWari is explicitly designed to solve this. Every lesson should demonstrate production-grade thinking, not tutorial-grade thinking.

### Signal 2: Trust is the product
Only 3% of developers highly trust AI tool output. The deeper need beneath every AI education purchase is not skill acquisition — it is *judgment acquisition*. The ability to know when the system is wrong, when the architecture will break, when the abstraction is leaking. Koda embodies this. Wari interrogates it. The learner watches them work it out.

### Signal 3: The senior developer is the highest-value learner
Counter-intuitively, experienced developers (10+ yrs) show the *most* distrust of AI tools and the most caution with complex tasks. They are the hardest to reach and the most valuable to convert — because when they endorse something, the rest of the market follows. KodaWari's register (compressed, aphoristic, respectful of prior knowledge) is already calibrated for this segment.

### Signal 4: Anime/manga aesthetic is a massive untapped trust signal
- Global anime market: $43.87B in 2025 → $114B by 2034 (CAGR 11.2%)
- LINE Manga: #1 grossing app in Japan H1 2025
- Research confirms: narrative + visual learning significantly reduces cognitive load and improves retention vs. lecture-only formats
- No competitor in the AI education space has claimed this aesthetic register. It is entirely open.

### Signal 5: Production failure is the curriculum
The #1 complaint in every forum, every hiring post, every course review: "The demo worked. The production system didn't." This is not a skill gap. It is a *systems thinking* gap. KodaWari's curriculum should be structured around the taxonomy of production failure modes — and how master engineers anticipate, diagnose, and resolve them.

## SECTION 5: STRATEGIC REFACTORING RECOMMENDATIONS

Based on this research, the following refactors are recommended to maximize product-market fit:

### REFACTOR 1: Reframe the ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)
**Current framing:** "Learn multi-agent AI design"
**Refactored framing:** "Develop the judgment of a senior AI systems engineer"

The market has plenty of places to *learn*. It has nowhere to *develop judgment*. Judgment is the product. Everything else is the vehicle.

### REFACTOR 2: Make the curriculum explicitly production-failure-driven
Each week's lesson should be anchored to a specific class of production failure:
- Week 1: The agent that works in dev and breaks in prod
- Week 2: The orchestration that scales to 10 and dies at 100
- Week 3: The memory system that poisons itself over time
- Week 4: The human-in-the-loop that creates more errors than it catches
...and so on. Koda has seen every one of these. Wari knows why it happened before he does.

### REFACTOR 3: Segment pricing by depth, not by time
**Current model:** 12-week cohort
**Refactored model:**
- **Dojo Access** (self-paced, manga panels + exercises): $49/month or $399/year
- **Sparring** (cohort, live sessions, Koda/Wari narrative chapters): $1,200/cohort
- **Ronin** (1:1 architecture review, personalized critique): $3,000-5,000

This mirrors the market's actual willingness-to-pay tiers and creates a natural upgrade path.

### REFACTOR 4: Ship the manga panels as the marketing
Every competitor markets with text, testimonials, and feature lists. KodaWari's marketing *is* the product — the panels themselves. Release one canonical panel per week on LinkedIn, X, and Instagram. Each panel teaches one real concept. Caption: the dialogue. No "sign up now." Just the panel. Let it speak.

The manga panels are the top of funnel. They are the proof of quality. They are the brand. Nothing else needs to be said.

### REFACTOR 5: Target the mid-career crisis explicitly
The most motivated, highest-paying learner is a developer with 5-10 years of experience who just realized that their entire mental model of software systems needs to be rebuilt for the agentic era. They are not looking for a course. They are looking for a *guide* — someone who has already made the crossing and can show them how.

Koda is that guide. He crossed it alone. The curriculum is the map he would have wanted.

### REFACTOR 6: Build the community before the course
The highest-retention ed-tech products (Duolingo, Maven cohorts, BuildSpace in its prime) are communities first, curriculum second. KodaWari should launch with:
- A Discord or Circle community called **The Dojo**
- Weekly panel drops with discussion threads
- A "sparring" channel where engineers post architecture decisions and get Koda/Wari-framed critique
- The cohort launches *into* an existing community — not to build one from scratch

### REFACTOR 7: The Wari voice is the marketing voice
Wari's questions are precision instruments. They make you feel the gap in your own thinking. Every marketing touchpoint should end with a Wari question — not a CTA.

Not: "Enroll now and learn multi-agent architecture."
But: *"You've described what your agent does. Have you described what it does when it fails?"*

That question is worth more than any conversion copy ever written.
