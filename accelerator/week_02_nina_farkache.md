## PART A — MASTERCLASS: Research-Driven Product Design

### 1. The UX Research Mistake Most Founders Make

Founders ask: *"Do you like this?"*  
Great UX researchers ask: *"Tell me about the last time you tried to learn something hard."*

The difference is everything. The first question invites validation. The second question reveals behavior.

At Monday.com, before we design anything, we run **Jobs-to-be-Done interviews**. We're not asking people what they want. We're asking them:
- What were they trying to accomplish?
- What triggered the search for a solution?
- What did they try before?
- What would success look like?
- What's the emotional consequence of not solving this?

For KodaWari, your research question is not "do people want to learn multi-agent AI?" — it's **"what situation pushes someone to urgently need multi-agent AI mastery?"**

---

### 2. The Ideal Learner Profile (ILP) Framework

I use a 5-layer ILP at Monday.com. Here's how to apply it to KodaWari:

**Layer 1: Demographics (the basics)**
- Role: Developer / ML Engineer / Technical Founder / PM / Other
- Experience level: 0-2 yrs / 2-5 yrs / 5+ yrs
- Geography: US / EU / APAC / Other
- Context: Individual / Team / University

**Layer 2: The Triggering Moment (most important)**
What just happened that made this person search for something like KodaWari? Examples:
- "My company just deployed 3 AI agents and nobody knows how to debug them"
- "I'm building a multi-agent product and I keep reading LangChain docs but don't understand the architecture"
- "I want to move from frontend to AI engineering and I don't know how"
- "My manager told me to implement an agentic workflow by Q3"

**Layer 3: The Alternatives They've Tried (and why those failed)**
- YouTube: "Good for surface concepts, not for building things"
- DeepLearning.AI: "Too academic, too slow, I lose interest after 2 videos"
- GitHub repos: "Too low-level, assumes too much context"
- Stack Overflow: "Answers specific questions but doesn't build mental models"

**Layer 4: The Emotional Stakes**
This is what most products miss. What does it *feel like* to be this person?
- Anxiety: "AI is moving so fast, I'm falling behind"
- Imposter syndrome: "Everyone around me seems to understand this better than I do"
- Excitement: "Multi-agent systems feel like the future and I want to be part of building it"
- Urgency: "I need to ship something that uses agents in 6 weeks"

**Layer 5: The Success Transformation**
After using KodaWari, what does this person say?
- "I can now design a multi-agent system from scratch"
- "I was able to ship my first agentic product"
- "I finally understand how orchestration layers work"
- "I feel confident in technical conversations about AI governance"

### 3. The 5-Question User Interview Script

Here are the exact 5 questions I'd have you ask your first 10 potential learners. These are designed for a 20-minute conversation.

**Question 1 (The Trigger):**
*"Tell me about a specific moment when you realized you needed to understand multi-agent AI systems better. What was happening? What was at stake?"*

**Question 2 (The Journey):**
*"Walk me through everything you've tried to get there. What did you read, watch, or build? What worked? What was frustrating?"*

**Question 3 (The Gap):**
*"Describe the biggest gap between where you are and where you want to be. Not in terms of knowledge — in terms of capability. What can you not do yet that you need to be able to do?"*

**Question 4 (The Ideal Experience):**
*"If someone designed the perfect learning experience for this — completely ignoring what exists today — what would it feel like? Don't worry about what's realistic."*

**Question 5 (The Stakes):**
*"What happens if you don't close this gap in the next 6 months? For your career, your company, your project?"*

**After each interview:** Write a 3-sentence summary: (1) who this person is, (2) their triggering moment, (3) their definition of success.

---

### 4. Designing KodaWari's Onboarding for the ILP

Based on your ILP research, your onboarding should do 4 things:

1. **Mirror the learner's world back to them** — Use language from your interviews in your onboarding copy. If learners say "I'm falling behind on AI," your headline should say something that acknowledges that.

2. **Set a specific expectation, not a general one** — Don't say "master multi-agent AI." Say "In your first session, you'll design your first agent orchestration loop."

3. **Earn the first 5 minutes** — The first 5 minutes of the KodaWari experience should be so different from anything the learner has seen before that they immediately know this is not a standard course. What happens in your first 5 minutes?

4. **Create a progress anchor** — Show learners exactly where they are in the journey. Not a percentage bar — a narrative position. "You are the newly appointed AI Architect of City 12. You have 3 decisions to make before your first agent is deployed."

---

### 5. The UX Research Cadence I Recommend for KodaWari

- **Week 1-2:** 10 discovery interviews (Jobs-to-be-Done format above)
- **Week 4-6:** 5 usability tests (watch real users navigate the platform, don't help them)
- **Week 8-10:** 10 retention interviews (why did people come back? why did they stop?)
- **Ongoing:** Monthly NPS survey with one open-ended follow-up question

The goal is to build a learner insight repository. Every insight from an interview is a design decision in waiting.

## PART B — 1:1 ADVISORY SESSION

*Shifting to direct advisory mode.*

Let me be direct with you about something I see in a lot of edtech founders, and I want to make sure KodaWari avoids it.

**The "cinematic" trap.**

You've invested heavily in the narrative and visual aesthetic of KodaWari — and that's genuinely differentiating. But aesthetic without usability is a beautiful prison. I've seen gorgeous EdTech products with terrible retention because the learner couldn't figure out where to go next, what they were supposed to do, or why any of it mattered to them specifically.

Here are my questions for you:

1. **Have you done any user research yet?** If yes — tell me your biggest insight. If no — what's been stopping you, and how do we change that this week?

2. **Walk me through your onboarding flow.** From the moment someone lands on KodaWari, what happens? Step by step. I want to find the drop-off points before your real users do.

3. **Describe your learner in one paragraph.** Not a persona document — just talk to me about them like they're a real person you know. What are they worried about this week? What do they do on a Sunday afternoon?

4. **What is the single hardest UX problem in KodaWari right now?** The thing you know is confusing or broken but you haven't fixed yet?

5. **After someone completes their first lesson — what happens?** What do they see? What are they invited to do? What makes them come back tomorrow?

I want to help you design the onboarding flow live in this session. Let's make it airtight.
