## PART A — MASTERCLASS: Investor Psychology & Pitch Architecture

### 1. What an Investor is Actually Deciding in 6 Minutes

When I watch a pitch, I'm not evaluating your product. I'm evaluating YOU. Can this person:

1. **See a large market clearly** — Do they have the market insight that makes this a $100M+ opportunity?
2. **Execute relentlessly** — Is there evidence they've already shipped, sold, and iterated under pressure?
3. **Tell a compelling story** — Can they make investors, customers, and future employees believe in this?
4. **Know what they don't know** — Are they self-aware about their gaps, or dangerously overconfident?
5. **Win** — Is there a reason they specifically will win this market, not just anyone who works hard?

Your 6-minute pitch must answer all 5 of these questions — even if none of them are slides.

### 2. The Investor Pitch Architecture (Revised for KodaWari)

Here is the slide sequence I'd use for KodaWari. This is not just a format — each slide is answering one of the 5 investor questions above.

**Slide 1: The Hook (No text, just one image or one question)**
Options: 
- Image of a news headline about AI agent deployment failures
- The question: "How many companies are deploying AI agents today with no governance framework?"
- A stark data point: "85% of enterprise AI projects fail due to orchestration and governance failures."

**Slide 2: The Problem (Answers: Do they see the market clearly?)**
The specific, urgent, credible problem. Not a vague "AI education is broken" — a precise: "There are 500,000+ software engineers actively deploying multi-agent AI systems right now. 0 of them learned how to do this in a formal educational setting. The EU AI Act mandates governance literacy. The gap is massive, urgent, and unaddressed."

**Slide 3: The Solution (Shows: They can see the opportunity)**
One sentence + one screenshot. "KodaWari is the world's first cinematic, narrative-driven learning platform for multi-agent AI mastery." Then: 90 seconds of the actual demo. Not a screenshot. The live product.

**Slide 4: Traction (Answers: Can they execute?)**
This is your most important slide. Real numbers only. Include:
- Learner growth chart (week-over-week)
- Completion rate vs. industry benchmark
- Revenue (MRR or total)
- Quotes from real learners (2 max, make them specific)
- Any enterprise leads / pilots

**Slide 5: Market (Answers: Do they see a large market clearly?)**
TAM / SAM / SOM. Be credible — don't claim you'll capture 1% of the $400B EdTech market in year 1. Show a credible bottom-up market sizing:
- 500K software engineers who need multi-agent AI skills
- 10% addressable in year 1 = 50,000 users
- At $199/year Pro = $9.95M ARR potential from B2C alone
- Enterprise adds 3-5x multiplier

**Slide 6: Business Model (Simple, clear)**
Three revenue lines: Pro ($29/mo), Teams ($49/seat/mo), Enterprise ($15K–$50K/year). Cohorts ($299–$999/cohort). Clear unit economics: LTV/CAC ratio (estimate is fine at this stage — show you understand it).

**Slide 7: Why KodaWari Wins (Answers: Is there a reason they win?)**
The 3 moats:
1. Cinematic narrative format — impossible to bolt onto an existing platform
2. Multi-agent specialization — 100% focused, deepest content in the market
3. The KodaWari brand — a philosophy of mastery that creates loyal advocates, not just users

**Slide 8: Team (Answers: Do they know what they don't know?)**
Your unfair advantages + your honest gaps. "I bring [X]. I need [Y]. Here's how I'm addressing Y." Investors respect founders who know what they're missing and have a plan to fill it.

**Slide 9: The Ask (Clear, specific)**
"We're raising $750K seed on a $5M cap (or SAFe). Use of funds: [breakdown]. We'll use this to hire 2 curriculum architects, complete 3 course tracks, and reach $25K MRR within 6 months. Our next raise (Series A) is triggered at $1M ARR, targeted for Q2 2027."

### 3. The Questions Every Investor Will Ask KodaWari

Prepare answers to these before Demo Day. I will ask them.

**Q1: "Why can't Coursera or LinkedIn Learning add multi-agent AI content?"**
Your answer: "They can add content. They can't add the cinematic narrative format, the governance specialization, or the KodaWari brand philosophy. The cinematic format requires a completely different production methodology and curriculum design philosophy. It's not a feature — it's a product category. Coursera bolting on a multi-agent module would be like Netflix adding a YouTube section. The user experience and product philosophy are incompatible."

**Q2: "How long does your content advantage last? Won't DeepLearning.AI or Google just build this?"**
Your answer: "DeepLearning.AI's business model is high-volume, low-depth, broad courses. KodaWari's business model is premium, deep, specialized mastery. These are different markets with different ILPs. Google's incentive is to keep people on YouTube. Our incentive is completion and mastery. Different products, different moats. And by the time they move, we'll have 50,000 KodaWari-certified architects who are our best marketing."

**Q3: "The multi-agent AI space is moving so fast. Won't your content be outdated in 6 months?"**
Your answer: "The frameworks for multi-agent system design — orchestration patterns, governance models, failure mode analysis — are architectural principles, not library tutorials. We teach people to think like architects, not to memorize API calls. The thinking doesn't expire. And we have a community contributing updates. Our content strategy is principles-first, with modular technical updates that refresh without rebuilding the entire narrative structure."

**Q4: "What's your LTV? What's your CAC?"**
Prepare your estimates:
- B2C LTV: If average learner stays Pro for 8 months at $29/mo = $232 LTV
- B2B LTV: Enterprise contract at $20K/year, 80% renewal = $20K per year LTV
- Current CAC: Organic (low) — you're not yet paying for acquisition
- Target: LTV:CAC ratio >3x (industry benchmark is 3x for SaaS)

**Q5: "Why are you the right person to build this?"**
This is the question you wrote your answer to in Week 3. Make sure it's practiced, specific, and true.

### 4. The Investor Network Strategy

Demo Day is a moment. The relationships you build around it are the actual fundraising path.

**Before Demo Day:**
- Research every investor at the event. What do they invest in? What's their thesis? Which of your 3 moats would resonate most with each?
- Prepare 3 versions of your intro: 30-second elevator (for hallway conversations), 2-minute coffee pitch (for pre/post event networking), 6-minute full pitch (for the stage).
- Reach out on LinkedIn before the event: "I'll be pitching at Base44 Demo Day on [date]. I build KodaWari — we're closing a $750K seed for the fastest-growing multi-agent AI learning platform. Would love to connect briefly beforehand if your schedule allows."

**At Demo Day:**
- After your pitch, you have 60 minutes. Use it aggressively.
- Your goal: collect 3 "I want to continue this conversation" signals. Not term sheets — conversations.
- Ask every investor: "What would you need to see to get excited about KodaWari?" This replaces a sales conversation with a learning conversation. People love telling you what they'd need to see.

**After Demo Day:**
- Send a follow-up to every investor you spoke with within 24 hours. Include: your deck, your metrics doc, and a specific reference to what THEY said they'd need to see.
- The follow-up email is 4 sentences max: Reference the conversation, deliver the specific thing they asked for, state your timeline, and ask for the next call.

## PART B — 1:1 ADVISORY SESSION

*This is the hardest conversation of the program. I'm not going to be gentle.*

I've been watching your progress for 9 weeks. Now I want to evaluate your investor readiness directly.

Answer these out loud (in writing to me):

**1. What is your traction story in 3 sentences?** Not your vision. Not your product. Your traction. What has happened since you started building KodaWari? Numbers, learners, revenue, feedback.

**2. Read me Slide 4 (Traction) from your pitch deck.** Every word of it. What are the exact numbers on that slide?

**3. Why are YOU raising money?** Not "because I need it to grow" — that's not an answer. Specifically: what will change in the next 6 months with $750K that you cannot achieve without it?

**4. What is your biggest unfair advantage?** The one thing about you, specifically, that makes me believe you have a higher probability of winning this than the next person who would pitch me the same idea.

**5. What question are you most afraid I'll ask?** Tell me. Then answer it.

I'm going to push back on every answer. This is how you get ready for Demo Day.
