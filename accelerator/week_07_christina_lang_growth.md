## PART A — MASTERCLASS: Growth Architecture for EdTech

### 1. The 3 Growth Engines and Which One Fits KodaWari

Every sustainable growth business is powered by one (or more) of 3 engines:

**Engine 1: Paid Growth** — You pay for users. Facebook Ads, Google Ads, sponsorships.
*Best for:* Products with proven LTV that justifies CAC. Not KodaWari yet.

**Engine 2: Viral / Word-of-Mouth Growth** — Users bring other users. Referral loops, social sharing, network effects.
*Best for:* Products where the experience itself is worth sharing. This is KodaWari.

**Engine 3: Content / SEO Growth** — You create valuable content that attracts users over time.
*Best for:* Products in large search categories. Slow burn but high quality leads. This is KodaWari too.

**For KodaWari in the next 90 days:** Focus 70% energy on Viral/WOM growth, 30% on Content growth. Zero on paid — your CAC payback period is unknown and your conversion funnel isn't optimized yet.

### 2. Engineering the KodaWari Viral Loop

A viral loop has 3 components:

1. **A moment of delight** — something in the product experience that makes the user want to share
2. **A frictionless sharing mechanism** — an easy, obvious way to share in that moment
3. **A landing that converts** — the person who receives the share converts into a user

**KodaWari's viral loop design:**

**Delight Moment:** The KodaWari Certification completion screen. After a learner earns their "KodaWari Certified: Multi-Agent Systems" credential — that's the highest-emotion moment in their journey. They've done something hard. They want to tell people.

**Sharing Mechanism:**
- One-click LinkedIn post generator: pre-written post + custom certificate image they can post
- Tweet/X template: "Just earned my KodaWari Certified: Multi-Agent Systems credential. If you're building with AI agents and want to go deep — this is the best learning experience I've found. [link]"
- Discord badge: learners who complete display a special "Architect" role in community

**Landing That Converts:**
- The certificate link (public, shareable) lands on a page showing the learner's name + what they built in their capstone project
- Below the certificate: "Become a KodaWari Certified Architect → [Start Free]"
- This turns every certificate into a mini landing page that converts the recipient's network

**Target viral coefficient (K-factor):** If each completing learner refers 0.4 new learners (40% of completers share and convert at 10%), every 100 completers → 40 new learners → 16 more completers → 6 more... This is a compounding loop. Even a K-factor of 0.3 is meaningful.

### 3. The Content Growth Strategy

Content takes 3-6 months to compound. Start now.

**The KodaWari Content Pillars:**
1. **Technical depth** — Original frameworks for multi-agent AI design (stuff you can't find on YouTube)
2. **Founder transparency** — Your building journey (what you're learning, what's working, what broke)
3. **Community amplification** — Learner stories, mission reports, graduate showcases

**High-leverage content formats for your ILP:**

*Twitter/X:* Technical threads. "How to design a multi-agent orchestration pipeline from scratch (a thread):" → 8-12 tweet thread with original diagrams. These get bookmarked and shared by technical audiences.

*LinkedIn:* "From the field" posts. "I just watched 20 AI teams try to deploy multi-agent systems. Here's the mistake almost all of them make:" → Story post with a practical insight. Tags enterprise buyers, developers, founders.

*YouTube/Loom:* 5-10 min deep dives. "The 3 governance patterns every multi-agent system needs" or "I compared 4 multi-agent frameworks — here's what I found." These rank for high-intent search queries.

**Posting cadence:**
- Twitter/X: 1 post per day (can be short observations, not just threads)
- LinkedIn: 3 posts per week (2 short, 1 long-form)
- YouTube/Loom: 1 video per week (this is high-leverage — don't skip it)

### 4. The Live Cohort as a Growth Engine

This week, you're launching your first live cohort. This is not just a revenue play — it's a growth mechanic.

**Why live cohorts drive growth:**
1. Cohort learners have higher completion rates (accountability effect)
2. Cohort learners form relationships — they become your community core
3. Cohort completers are the most likely to share publicly (they're proud, they did it together)
4. Cohort programs are highly sharable on LinkedIn ("Just completed Week 4 of KodaWari's Multi-Agent Systems cohort...")

**Live Cohort v1 Design (4 weeks, 20 learners, $299):**
- Week 1: Orientation + Foundations of Agentic Systems
- Week 2: Orchestration Architecture (with peer discussion)
- Week 3: Governance & Safety Frameworks
- Week 4: Capstone Presentations (learners present their designs to the group)

**Where to find your first 20 cohort members:**
- Your "50 Founding Architects" community (offer them a discount: $199 for founding members)
- LinkedIn post: "I'm running a 4-week live cohort for engineers who want to go deep on multi-agent AI. 20 spots. Starts [date]. DM me." (Don't post a link first — DMs create conversation, conversations convert better)
- AI Discord communities: Post in #jobs-and-opportunities or #general channels

You are building KodaWari. You have opinions about multi-agent AI. You are living the problem your learners face. But if you're not sharing that publicly, you are leaving the most powerful growth channel completely untapped.

Here's what I'd do this week if I were you:

That one post, done well, could bring you 50 qualified learners. I've seen founders generate 500 signups from a single thread. The formula is: be genuinely useful + give more than you ask for + let the product follow naturally.
