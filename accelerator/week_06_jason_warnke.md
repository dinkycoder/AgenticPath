## PART A — MASTERCLASS: B2B Sales Motion for EdTech

### 1. The Enterprise Buyer's World

Let me describe your target enterprise buyer. She's a Learning & Development Director at a company with 500+ engineers. Her AI teams are scrambling to deploy multi-agent systems because the CEO read something about AI agents in the FT and now everyone is panicking. She has:

- A budget: probably $50K–$200K for AI upskilling this year
- A mandate: get the engineering teams capable with agentic AI by Q4
- A constraint: limited time to vet vendors, limited patience for demos that don't get to the point
- A fear: recommending something that doesn't work, that her teams hate, that doesn't show ROI

She is not your learner. She is your buyer. These are different people with different needs.

**Your learner asks:** "Will I learn something I can actually use?"  
**Your buyer asks:** "Can I justify this purchase to my CFO and does it come with reporting?"

---

### 2. The B2B Value Proposition Reframe

KodaWari's consumer value proposition: *"A cinematic learning experience that makes multi-agent AI mastery engaging."*

KodaWari's enterprise value proposition: *"An AI governance and orchestration upskilling platform that increases your team's multi-agent deployment capability in 8 weeks — with completion rates 3x the industry average and reporting dashboards that prove ROI to your leadership."*

Notice what changed:
- "Cinematic" is replaced with "governance and orchestration" (enterprise language)
- "Engaging" is replaced with "8 weeks" and "3x completion rates" (measurable outcomes)
- "Learner experience" is replaced with "reporting dashboards that prove ROI" (buyer language)

The product is identical. The framing is completely different. This is the B2B reframe.

### 3. The Enterprise Pilot Structure

Enterprises don't buy at scale from unknown vendors. They pilot. Here's the enterprise pilot structure I'd recommend for KodaWari:

**Pilot Terms:**
- Duration: 8 weeks
- Cohort size: 15–25 engineers
- Price: $2,500–$5,000 (low enough to be a single-decision purchase, high enough to signal value)
- Deliverables to buyer: Weekly cohort progress reports, final completion rate, learner NPS, before/after capability assessment
- KodaWari commitment: Dedicated Slack channel with founder access, weekly check-in call, customized report deck at end

**What the pilot gives KodaWari:**
1. A paying reference customer (invaluable for enterprise sales)
2. Real enterprise data on completion rates and engagement
3. A case study you can use in every subsequent enterprise pitch
4. A relationship with the buyer that converts to an annual contract if results are good

**How to price the full contract after pilot:**
- Per seat: $49/seat/month (minimum 20 seats = $980/month, $11,760/year)
- Cohort license: $15,000–$30,000/year for up to 100 users
- Enterprise license: $50,000+/year for unlimited users + white-label + custom content

### 4. The 5-Step Enterprise Sales Motion

**Step 1: Find the Buyer**
LinkedIn is your primary channel. Search: "L&D Director" + "AI" + "Enterprise" or "Head of Engineering Enablement" + "[company name]". Target companies with >100 engineers that are publicly investing in AI (check their job postings — if they're hiring AI engineers, they need AI training).

**Step 2: The Cold Outreach (NOT a product pitch)**
The worst thing you can do is lead with your product. Lead with their pain.

*Example outreach:*
> "Hi [Name], I noticed [Company] is scaling its AI engineering team — particularly in agentic systems. Most companies I speak with are running into the same challenge: their teams can deploy agents but struggle with orchestration patterns and governance. I built KodaWari specifically for this gap. Would a 15-minute conversation be worth your time? I'll bring 2 specific frameworks we've seen help teams like yours."

This works because: it references their world, names a specific pain, offers value, and asks for almost nothing (15 minutes).

**Step 3: The Discovery Call**
Your job on this call is NOT to pitch. Your job is to ask 5 questions:
1. "How is your team currently getting up to speed on multi-agent AI?"
2. "What's the gap between where they are and where they need to be?"
3. "What would success look like for an AI upskilling program in your org?"
4. "What's the timeline pressure? What's driving the urgency?"
5. "Who else would be involved in a decision like this?"

Then shut up and listen. The answers will tell you exactly how to position KodaWari.

**Step 4: The Pilot Proposal**
After the discovery call, send a 1-page pilot proposal within 24 hours. It should include:
- What KodaWari solves for their specific pain (use their words from the call)
- The 8-week pilot structure
- The price
- The success metrics they'll be able to show their leadership

**Step 5: Close and Deliver**
Enterprise buyers move slowly unless you create urgency. After sending the proposal, follow up in 3 days: "We're setting up our Q3 enterprise cohorts — I wanted to make sure you had first access before we fill those slots." This is true and creates appropriate urgency.

---

### 5. The 3 Enterprise Segments to Target First

**Segment 1: AI-First Startups (50–200 employees)**
- These teams are building with multi-agent systems RIGHT NOW
- Budget: $5,000–$15,000/year
- Decision maker: Head of Engineering or CTO
- Sales cycle: 2–4 weeks
- Why they buy: Their team needs to move fast and they don't have time to build training internally

**Segment 2: Consulting Firms with AI Practices**
- Accenture, Deloitte, McKinsey, BCG all have AI transformation practices
- Budget: $50,000–$200,000/year
- Decision maker: L&D Director or Practice Lead
- Sales cycle: 6–12 weeks
- Why they buy: Their consultants need credible AI governance frameworks to advise clients

**Segment 3: University CS/AI Departments**
- Budget: $5,000–$25,000/year
- Decision maker: Department Chair or Course Director
- Sales cycle: 3–6 months (academic procurement is slow)
- Why they buy: Modern AI curriculum that's actually engaging — professors know their students are bored with passive video

## PART B — 1:1 ADVISORY SESSION

*Switching to direct advisory mode.*

Let me be honest with you. Most edtech founders I meet have never made an enterprise sales call in their life. They know how to build products, they know how to talk to individual learners — but the enterprise motion is a completely different discipline.

Here's what I want to know:

1. **Have you made any B2B outreach yet?** Even an informal conversation with someone who works in L&D at a company? Tell me what happened.

2. **Who is your warmest enterprise lead?** Is there a company, a person, a LinkedIn connection that is closer to a buying conversation than anyone else? Who is it and what's the relationship?

3. **What is your current enterprise-facing messaging?** Go to your KodaWari homepage or marketing material right now. Is there anything on it that speaks to an L&D buyer? Or is it entirely consumer-facing?

4. **What would a 15-pilot quarter look like for KodaWari?** Map it out: 15 companies, 15–25 learners each = 225–375 enterprise learners. At $3,500 per pilot, that's $52,500 in quarterly revenue. Is that plausible in 90 days? What's the bottleneck?

5. **What's your unfair advantage in enterprise sales?** Do you have a network? A credibility signal? A past employer in the relevant industry? We need to find your natural wedge into enterprise accounts.
