## PANEL EVALUATION CRITERIA

Each faculty member will evaluate you on their area of expertise. Here is exactly what each is looking for:

---

### RAPHAEL LAUFER — Product

Questions I'll be asking myself as you pitch:
- Is the product actually different, or just different-looking?
- Does the demo show a working, end-to-end experience with real users?
- Do you know your core metrics cold?
- Can I see the Base44 platform being used to its full leverage?

**What will impress me:** A clean, working demo with real traction data. Specific product decisions defended with user research.

**What will concern me:** A demo that crashes, a founder who doesn't know their completion rate, a product that looks polished but has no users.

---

### NINA FARKACHE — UX Research & Learner Experience

Questions I'll be asking myself:
- Did this founder talk to real users, or are they building for imaginary ones?
- Is the onboarding experience research-backed?
- Do they know where their learners drop off?

**What will impress me:** Specific user interview insights cited in the pitch. Drop-off data that drove a specific product decision. A first-5-minutes experience that is genuinely different.

**What will concern me:** Generic ILP ("developers who want to learn AI"), no user research data, an onboarding flow that assumes learners already know what they want.

---

### YUVAL GEFFEN — HCI & Investor Lens

Questions I'll be asking as an investor AND as an HCI specialist:
- Is the interaction model defensible at scale?
- Does the demo create a "felt sense" of value in under 2 minutes?
- Can I answer "why can't Coursera just copy this?" with a satisfying answer?

**What will impress me:** A demo that makes me wish I were a learner. A clear articulation of the 3 moats. Polished UX that signals craft.

**What will concern me:** A demo that shows slides more than product. An inability to articulate the competitive moat. UX that contradicts the "relentless craft" brand promise.

---

### JORDANA DUMONT — Retention & User Behavior

Questions I'll be asking:
- Do they have a retention strategy, or are they hoping learners come back?
- What does their Day 1 return rate look like?
- Have they designed for the 4 micro-moments?

**What will impress me:** Completion rate meaningfully above industry average (>25%). A narrative-driven re-engagement strategy. Specific retention data from real users.

**What will concern me:** No data on retention. Assuming high completion because the content is good. No re-engagement mechanism.

---

### CHRISTINA LANG — Growth & Community

Questions I'll be asking:
- Is there a viral loop built into the product?
- Is the founder building in public?
- Is there a community, or just an email list?

**What will impress me:** Evidence of organic sharing (screenshots of learners posting about KodaWari). A live community with active members. A content strategy that's already working.

**What will concern me:** No community presence. No viral mechanism. A founder who is building in stealth. Zero external signals of demand.

---

### JASON WARNKE — Enterprise & B2B

Questions I'll be asking:
- Does this product have a credible enterprise sales motion?
- Have they talked to an L&D buyer?
- Is the B2B value proposition articulated separately from the B2C value prop?

**What will impress me:** A signed or in-progress enterprise pilot. A B2B one-pager that speaks enterprise language. Evidence that an L&D buyer would pay for this.

**What will concern me:** No enterprise outreach. A pitch that is 100% B2C. No understanding of how enterprise procurement works.

---

### J. SKYLER FERNANDES — Investment Readiness

Questions I'll be asking:
- Is this a venture-scale business?
- Does the founder understand their unit economics?
- Am I watching someone who can execute for 10 years on this?

**What will impress me:** A clean financial model. Specific use of funds. A "why you" story that is compelling and credible. A traction story that shows early PMF.

**What will concern me:** Vague financials ("we'll grow fast"). An inability to defend the $750K ask. A pitch that focuses more on vision than execution. No sense of the founder's personal stake in this.
