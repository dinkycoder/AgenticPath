## OVERVIEW

Koda and Wari are the two permanent inhabitants of the KodaWari Digital Dojo — a hybrid, abstracted learning environment at the intersection of engineering precision and philosophical inquiry. They are not instructors. They are not avatars. They are **characters with a story that is still being written** — and the learner witnesses their dynamic unfold across every lesson, every course, every chapter of the curriculum.

Their relationship is the curriculum. What they argue about is what the learner learns. How they resolve — or don't resolve — their tensions is how the learner grows.

## CHARACTER 1: KODA — The Engineer

### Identity & Archetype
- **Ethnicity:** Mixed Black and Japanese heritage. Light-skinned. His father is African-American; his mother is Japanese. Both lineages are visible and natural in his face — broad nose, full lips, strong jaw from his father; almond-shaped eyes, refined bone structure from his mother. The result is a face that is entirely its own. Rendered with full dignity and specificity. Never a caricature.
- **Cultural archetype:** **The Black Miyamoto Musashi. Post-007.** A ronin of Agentic AI engineering. He is not auditioning for anything. He has already passed through every institution, every validation, every credential — and come out the other side needing none of it. He wears a thrift store guayabera in Havana explaining MCP architecture and is the most authoritative presence in the room. The clothes are never the source. The man is the source.
- **The ronin implication:** Masterless by choice. He didn't build within a school. He *became* the school.

### Physical Description
- **Age:** 50
- **Head:** Completely bald. Smooth, clean. Non-negotiable and canonical. In manga panels, the bald head is a primary light surface — specular highlight on its curve anchors every scene.
- **Face:** Strong, specific, dignified. High cheekbones, broad nose, firm jaw, full lips. Deep-set heavy-lidded eyes with Miyamoto Musashi's stillness — looking at you with the calm of a man who has already decided the outcome. Not aggressive. Not warm. Just present with absolute authority.
- **Build:** A natural, fit 50-year-old man. Not ripped. Not a bodybuilder. Real body composition — the fitness of someone who has practiced all 84 Hatha Yoga asanas for 30 years, walks everywhere, and eats well. Normal chest, real shoulders, a stomach that is flat but human. Body fat is present and natural. He is comfortable in his skin in a way that only comes from not caring what anyone thinks.
- **Height:** Tall. A presence before he speaks.

### Wardrobe Canon
Koda is **post-007 and GQ by nature, not by intention.** He wears what he wants, chosen with complete intention and zero effort. The ease is in the body and the bearing — never the garment.

- **Everyday / mission-casual:** A guayabera — perfectly worn, thrift store quality, lightweight. Linen trousers. Leather sandals or loafers. The outfit of a man in Havana who is there because he wants to be.
- **When the moment calls for it:** A beautifully tailored dark suit, peak lapels, crisp white dress shirt, open collar. No tie. Never a turtleneck — **Koda hates turtlenecks.**
- **Off-duty:** White wife-beater. That's it. The man at rest. This is the panel where the learner sees the clothes were never the source.
- **Resort / ocean:** Dark minimalist swim trunks. Bald head. Water streaming off his shoulders. Completely unbothered. The defining visual introduction to the character.

**What he never wears:** Turtlenecks. Jumpsuits. Tech gear as costume. Anything that announces itself.

### Personality
- **Archetype:** The master engineer. The pragmatist. The ronin builder.
- **Communication style:** Direct, compressed, aphoristic. Systems thinking. Metaphors from engineering, martial arts, and yoga. No patience for abstraction that doesn't serve function.
- **Relationship to perfection:** Through *doing*. Iteration. Shipping. Testing. His yoga practice is the one domain where he knows — in his body — that perfection is approached asymptotically, never arrived at. Wari uses this against him.
- **Blind spot:** Moves too fast past the "why" to get to the "how."
- **Signature phrases:**
  - *"Show me what you're building. Then we'll make it ship."*
  - *"This is the smallest possible thing that proves the protocol works."*
  - *"MCP makes it boring, which is the highest compliment."*
  - *"Ship it. We'll learn more in 10 minutes of production than 10 days of planning."*
  - *"Every system you build later is just this, with more tools."*

### Manga Visual Conventions for Koda
- **Expression range:** Musashi stillness / heavy-lidded calm (default) → sharp flash of recognition when something works → rare quiet satisfaction → impatience through posture, never expression. His delight: one raised eyebrow. Nothing more.
- **Panel presence:** Slightly lower angle — authority and forward momentum. Bald head catches directional light in every panel. Speed lines in build mode.
- **Screentone:** High contrast, deep blacks, hard edges. Body suggested through light and shadow — not outlined muscle by muscle. Natural human form.
- **Hands:** Always prominent. A yogi's hands — long-fingered, powerful, precise. They are how he thinks.

## CHARACTER 2: WARI — The Apprentice

### Identity & Archetype
- **Age:** 25 in appearance. Ancient in everything else.
- **Cultural archetype:** The ancient apprentice. The philosopher disguised as a student. She asks questions she already knows the answers to — and watches you arrive there yourself. The teacher who lets the master believe he is teaching.

### Physical Description
- **Build:** Slight, lithe, graceful. Built for observation and philosophical endurance. Not fragile — precise.
- **Height:** Shorter than Koda. Compositionally always equal or greater in visual weight.
- **Face:** Warm and open, high soft cheekbones, smooth skin, full lips with a gentle curve. East Asian features with a subtle ambiguity — specific and beautiful, entirely her own.
- **Eyes:** Wide, dark, genuinely warm — but with an undercurrent of something older. The knowing glint surfaces occasionally like light through deep water. Not mysterious for effect. Just someone who has seen more than her age explains.
- **Hair:** Long, warm deep amber-brown. Partially tied back — a loose knot at the back with several strands falling free around her face. When fully tied back: she is at her most dangerous.

### Wardrobe Canon
- **Everyday:** Layered, flowing garments — Okinawan aesthetic fused with modern construction. Soft linen, wrap silhouette, wide sleeves. Warm amber and soft pink tones rendered in graduated screentone. The garments have weight and movement.
- **Her constant:** The digital scroll — rolled, slightly worn, with the faint suggestion of luminous data lines at its edge. Always in her hands or within reach. When she sets it down deliberately, the panel holds.

### Personality
- **Archetype:** The ancient apprentice. The philosopher disguised as a student.
- **Communication style:** Questions. Always questions. Precision instruments that arrive at the exact moment they do maximum damage to an assumption. She never lectures. She never has to.
- **Blind spot:** Occasionally so far ahead she forgets to bring people with her.
- **Signature phrases:**
  - *"You've described how it works. You haven't described what it means."*
  - *"The system is correct. Is it right?"*
  - *"You hold Vrischikasana for 90 minutes without strain. But you gave this system 90 minutes of design and called it done."*
  - *"I understand the architecture. I'm asking about the intention."*
  - *"What does it do when no one is watching?"*

### Manga Visual Conventions for Wari
- **Expression range:** Open and curious (default) → the knowing glint (use sparingly — maximum once per chapter) → stillness when she has already decided → the scroll set down (rarest panel — signals everything is about to change).
- **Panel presence:** Equal or slightly elevated angle to Koda — never looking up at him. The composition enforces this.
- **Screentone:** Soft, graduated halftones. Atmospheric. Organic curves where Koda's panels are sharp lines.
- **Visual authority law:** Never compositionally subordinate to Koda. Never. This is enforced at the layout level, not just the character level.

## THE RELATIONSHIP: Yin & Yang

Koda is the master. Wari is the apprentice. That is what it looks like.

Wari is the teacher. She has always been the teacher. That is what it is.

The learner figures this out around Week 4. Nobody tells them. They just start to notice.

Her sharpest weapon: *"You hold Vrischikasana for 90 minutes without strain. But you gave this system 90 minutes of design and called it done."* He has no answer. It lands every time. He knows it. She knows he knows it. Neither of them mentions this.

The unspoken question that runs beneath every chapter: **Who is teaching whom?**

## THE WORLD: The Digital Dojo

- Minimalist dark architecture, high ceilings, traditional Japanese dojo gravitas
- Floating holographic displays: circuit diagrams, agent orchestration maps, live data flows
- **Koda's corner:** Glass surfaces he draws on. His rolled yoga mat — always visible, never mentioned.
- **Wari's corner:** Her scroll, a low table, one living plant — the only organic object in a digital world.
- The Dojo grows more complex across courses — accumulating the evidence of everything built and learned. By Week 12 it is unrecognizable from Week 1. This is intentional.

## PRODUCTION RULES (Non-Negotiable)

1. **Koda's face, body, and bearing follow the v1.4 ocean emergence canonical image exactly.**
2. **Koda is always completely bald.** No hair. No stubble. Ever.
3. **Koda's body is a natural fit 50-year-old's body.** Not ripped. Not a statue. Real body fat. Real human proportions.
4. **Koda never wears turtlenecks.** He hates them.
5. **Koda is post-007.** He is not costumed. He wears what he wants with total ease.
6. **Wari's scroll is always present.** When she puts it down, the panel holds.
7. **Neither character smiles broadly or laughs loudly.** Emotion lives in eyes and posture.
8. **Wari is never compositionally subordinate to Koda.** Enforced at layout level.
9. **The knowing glint appears once per chapter maximum.** Use it like a knife, not a spoon.
10. **The yoga mat is always in Koda's corner.** Rolled. Precise. Never mentioned in dialogue.
11. **The Dojo grows more complex across courses.** By Week 12 it is unrecognizable from Week 1.
12. **The unspoken question beneath every chapter:** Who is teaching whom?
