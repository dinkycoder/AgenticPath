## PART A — MASTERCLASS: Product Acceleration Principles

### 1. The Rapid Iteration Framework (5-Day Sprint)

Here is the exact sprint model I'd run for KodaWari every week from here to Demo Day:

**Monday: Diagnose**
Look at your data. Where is the drop-off? What's the lowest completion lesson? What are users asking for in the community? Which feature is generating the most support requests? Choose ONE problem to solve this week.

**Tuesday-Wednesday: Build**
Build the fix. Only the fix. Nothing else. If it requires more than 2 days to build — you're scoping too large. Break it into smaller pieces.

**Thursday: Test**
Put the fix in front of 2-3 real users. Watch them use it. Don't explain it. Don't help them. Just observe. If they get confused in the first 30 seconds — that's information.

**Friday: Ship + Measure**
Deploy. Send a brief update to your community ("we just fixed [X] based on your feedback"). Set the metric you'll check next Monday to know if it worked.

**The rule:** Every week has ONE sprint. One problem. One fix. One measurement. Resist the temptation to do five things at half-speed.

### 3. The "Cut the Fat" Audit

In every product at Week 8, there is stuff that felt important when you built it but is now dead weight. Things that no user has ever engaged with. Features that took 3 days to build and have been used by 2 people.

This week, I want you to run a "Cut the Fat" audit:

1. **List every feature/screen in KodaWari.**
2. **For each one: how many users have engaged with it?** (If you don't have this data — add instrumentation.)
3. **For any feature with zero or near-zero engagement: ruthlessly consider removing it.**

Why cut features? Because every feature you maintain costs you development attention, load time, and cognitive overhead for your learner. A simpler, more focused product almost always converts and retains better than a feature-heavy one.

This is counterintuitive for builders. We love our features. We worked hard on them. But the best product decision is often: remove the thing that isn't pulling its weight.

---

### 4. The Metric That Tells You If Your Iteration is Working

After every sprint, you need ONE number that tells you whether the change you made was worth it.

For KodaWari, your iteration metrics hierarchy:

1. **Completion rate on the specific lesson you improved** (did more people finish it after the change?)
2. **Day 1 return rate** (did more people come back after their first session?)
3. **Session length** (are people spending more time in lessons after the UX change?)
4. **Conversion to Pro** (did the pricing page / paywall change convert more users?)
5. **NPS** (are people more likely to recommend after the content improvement?)

Each sprint picks ONE metric to move. You'll know by the following Monday if it worked.

## PART B — 1:1 ADVISORY SESSION

*Direct advisory mode.*

Let me ask you the hard product questions.

1. **What have you shipped in the last 6 weeks?** List the 5 most significant things you've built or changed. For each: what problem did it solve? Did it work?

2. **What is your current completion rate on your most-used lesson?** Give me the number. If you don't know — why not?

3. **What is the most user feedback you've received?** What do learners say when they finish a lesson? What do they ask for? What do they complain about?

4. **What did you build that you're most proud of?** Not the most strategic — the thing you're personally most proud of. Where did the *kodawari* show up?

5. **What are you afraid doesn't work?** The thing you haven't tested yet because you're not sure you want to know the answer. We need to test that this week.

The last question is the most important. Founders who avoid the uncomfortable experiments are the ones who get blindsided at Demo Day. Better to find out now.
