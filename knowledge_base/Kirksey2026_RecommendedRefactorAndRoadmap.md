## New Product Thesis

**KodaWari becomes an agent-engineering academy for dependable, production-grade AI systems.**

The cinematic layer stays, but moves from being the main product promise to being the **packaging system** for a more credible outcome promise.

Hero statement shifts from "immersive multi-agent dojo" to:
> **"Build, evaluate, and govern real AI agents — then prove it with labs, capstones, and team-ready reporting."**

---

## The MVP That Best Fits Demand

### Four flagship modules:
1. Agent architecture and tool use
2. Workflows, orchestration, memory, and context
3. Evaluation, observability, and failure analysis
4. Governance, guardrails, and enterprise deployment patterns

### MVP Feature Table:
| Feature | Rationale | Effort |
|---|---|---|
| One flagship learning path: Production Agent Engineering | Strongest overlap between employer demand, learner interest, and current curriculum assets | Medium |
| Hands-on labs in one opinionated stack + framework-neutral concepts | Market wants execution, not explanation; avoid teaching every framework at once | High |
| Built-in evals and observability module | Strongest market signal; generic courses are thinnest here | Medium |
| Capstone with rubric and public artifact | Converts learning into portfolio proof and hiring signal | Medium |
| Pre/post assessment and shareable credential | Essential for enterprise buyers and serious learners | Medium |
| Authentication, payments, analytics, event tracking | Core commercial plumbing | Medium |
| Enterprise pilot dashboard: seat progress, completion, assessment deltas | Necessary for pilot-to-annual conversion | High |

### MVP Exclusions (Phase 1):
- Creator marketplace
- Mobile app
- Broad course catalog
- Investor-readiness modules
- Open API for third parties
- Heavily branched story-world expansion

## Pricing and Monetization

| Motion | Offer | Suggested Price | Why It Fits |
|---|---|---|---|
| Free top of funnel | Intro lesson + skill diagnostic + sample lab | Free | Competes with free market, captures demand without friction |
| Individual subscription | Full flagship path + labs + capstone + credential | $49/month or $399/year | Higher than generic libraries; lower than bootcamps; justified by specialization |
| Premium cohort | 4–6 week live cohort with feedback and office hours | $900–$1,500/learner | Competes in "serious builder" tier |
| Enterprise pilot | 6–8 week program for 15–30 seats with manager reporting | $7,500–$15,000 | Low-friction pilot budget, signals seriousness |
| Annual enterprise | Team access, reporting, manager dashboards, custom cohorting | $25,000–$60,000+ | Aligns to L&D and enablement budgets |
| Channel licensing | Bootcamp/university licensing or white-labeled cohort kit | $10,000–$25,000 or rev-share | Turns slower-cycle partners into scalable distribution |

## Risk Matrix

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Aesthetic over substance | High | High | Make every module lab-first; instrument outcomes; lead marketing with capabilities, not lore |
| Framework churn | High | Medium-High | Teach one opinionated stack + framework-neutral principles; update quarterly |
| Weak proof of learning | Medium | High | Pre/post assessments, capstone rubric, manager reporting, completion analytics |
| Enterprise sales drag | Medium | High | Start with paid pilots, not annual deals; focus on AI-first teams and consultancies first |
| Content-production bottleneck | High | Medium | Use a compact flagship path before expanding to multiple tracks |
| Reliability of live agent labs | Medium | High | Design against fixed datasets, versioned tools, and controlled environments |

## Go-to-Market

**Most efficient motion: enterprise-first for revenue, learner-first for discovery.**

- **Enterprise:** Target AI-first product teams, enablement leads, consultancies, platform groups already standing up agent workflows. Use 8-week pilot structure. Sell buyer-facing outcomes. Replace generic "AI upskilling" language with: **agent orchestration, evals, and governance readiness in one compact program.**
- **Individual:** Content focused on agent failure modes, evals, governance, and production patterns. Articles, teardown posts, sample eval reports, mini-labs, "what actually breaks in agents" examples.
- **Partnerships:** Bootcamps and universities as distribution channels, not primary ICPs. Offer cohort kit, instructor guide, capstone rubric, partner license AFTER first flagship path works.

## Minimal Viable Measurement Plan

|---|---|---|
| Discovery | Visitor-to-email conversion | 5–8% |
| Activation | % of signups starting first lab | 35–50% |

| Learning | Pre/post assessment delta | Positive and statistically visible by cohort |
| Portfolio proof | Capstone submission rate | 15–25% of active paid learners |
| Monetization | Free-to-paid conversion | 3–7% self-serve; higher for cohorts |
| Enterprise | Pilot completion | 60%+ learner completion inside pilot |
| Enterprise | Pilot-to-annual expansion | 30%+ |
| Satisfaction | Learner NPS / manager NPS | 45+ |

## Central Conclusion

> The highest-confidence, least-waste refactor is to turn KodaWari from a broad cinematic academy into a **sharply outcome-oriented agent-engineering product with one flagship path, one enterprise pilot motion, and one proof-of-learning loop.**
