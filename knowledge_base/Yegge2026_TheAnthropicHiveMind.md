## Core Thesis

> *"The future of software development is the Hive Mind."*

Anthropic is operating the way **all successful companies will soon operate** — a self-organizing, vibe-driven, campfire model running on shortest possible feedback loops.

---

## Key Observations About Anthropic

### The Mood
- Vibrantly happy. Same crackle as Amazon 1998.
- "Sweetly but sadly transcendent" — shepherding something of civilization-level importance
- They feel genuinely sorry for companies not taking this seriously enough
- **2026 is going to break a lot of companies that don't see it coming**

### The Operating Model
- No operating plan ahead more than **90 days** (outermost planning cycle)
- Not run like any company of their size — no compartmentalization
- Described by employees as: **"A hive mind run entirely on vibes"**
- **"Yes, and…" style improvisational theater** — every idea welcomed, examined, judged by the hive
- No central decision-making authority. Just trying everything. When magic happens, everyone realizes it at once.

### The Golden Age Formula
> *"During Golden Ages, there is more work than people. When they crash, it is because there are more people than work."*

- **Google's mistake (April 2011):** Larry Page said "stop working on new stuff" → cut work by 50%+ → people fought over remaining work → political → empire-building → Golden Age ended
- **Amazon's advantage:** "Everyone here is always slightly oversubscribed" — infinite work generates luck
- **Anthropic now:** Surface of an expanding sphere. Infinite work. Everyone gets their chance.

## The Campfire Model

> *"Rather than a bunch of traditional departmental silos, they are building together around a campfire."*

- **Center of the campfire:** A living prototype — no waterfall, no spec
- The prototype simply evolves via group sculpting into the final product
- **Exploratory / Evolutionary Development** — you know it when you find it
- Claude Cowork: launched publicly **10 days** after first having the idea

## The SageOx Proof Point (3-person hive mind)

SageOx (Ajit Banerjee, Ryan Snodgrass, Milkana Brace) in Kirkland, WA:
- Alternating between coding and sleeping for weeks
- **Full transparency at all times** — entire workstream public, every conversation recorded, transcripts auto-uploaded and versioned
- Full work history of every human and agent, forever
- They course-correct each other in real time — whole team pair programming simultaneously
- The model: **"I AM ALSO DELETING THE DATABASE." "OK."**

> *"You need full transparency at all times, at their speeds, or nobody will ever see what you are doing and you'll fall irretrievably behind."*

**The death of the ego.** Everyone can see all your mistakes and wrong turns. You have to be a happy bee.

## Building for Yourself

> *"Building for yourself is the **only way** to give your product a nonzero chance of success in the new world. Build something just for yourself, and make sure you love it so much that you **know** it's how other people should be working."*

Too many AI-native founders building for enterprises with little agent workbenches, personas, helpers, RAG-like stuff — wrong side of the Bitter Lesson. They're not building for themselves, so they can't see it.

## The Yellow Brick Road

> *"There is a yellow brick road: spending tokens."*

- If you have a strictly online/SaaS presence with no atoms — **you are screwed if you don't pivot**
- The only way to know you're learning organizational lessons: people are out there trying and making mistakes
- You can tell how much practice they're getting from their **token spend**
